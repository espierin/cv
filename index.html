HLA Data Standards
Structure & Interoperability

Work on HLA data standards focuses on defining clear, compact formats for representing alleles, antigens, epitopes, and antibodies across laboratories and IT systems.

  • Development and refinement of PL- and AL-string representations.
  • Alignment with existing formats such as GL-strings and HML output.
  • Enabling unambiguous data exchange between registries, allocation systems, and labs.
  • Preparing formats for use in FHIR-based data exchange and modern APIs.
Diagnostic Innovation
From Bench to Report

Diagnostic innovation connects novel immunological concepts with the concrete reality of clinical reporting, quality standards, and turnaround times.

  • Embedding epitope-based risk scores in routine HLA and antibody reporting.
  • Designing decision-support outputs for transplant physicians.
  • Continuous refinement of workflows for HLA typing, antibody screening, and DSA analysis.
  • Integration of new assays while maintaining ISO15189/EFI compliance.
Research & Implementation
Bridging Lab & Clinic

Research and implementation work ensures that concepts such as epitope matching and data standards translate into measurable clinical benefit.

  • Design and support of studies on graft survival, rejection, and donor-specific antibodies.
  • Use of simulation studies to test allocation strategies before clinical rollout.
  • Collaboration with international partners to benchmark and harmonize approaches.
  • Implementation projects with transplant centers and donor registries.
